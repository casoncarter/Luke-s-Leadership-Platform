Executive Summary

Learning guitar as a pianist isn’t starting over — it’s unlocking music from a new angle. If piano is architecture, guitar is geometry: shapes, patterns, and repeating structures that turn harmony into something physical and immediate. Because Luke already understands chords, keys, progressions, and ear-based learning, he can learn guitar much faster than the average beginner.

This platform teaches guitar the way musicians actually use it:

Shape-based (CAGED) rather than notation-based

Ear-first, not theory-first

Song-driven, not exercise-driven

Play-focused, not practice-heavy

By mastering a few core concepts — chord shapes, transitions, strumming, fretboard patterns, and song structure — Luke can quickly gain fluency and freedom on the instrument.

This is the path not only to playing the guitar, but to enjoying it.

Full Module Content

Module 1

The Guitar Mindset — Play by Shape, Not by Note

Core Principle: Piano thinks vertically; guitar thinks geometrically.

On piano, chords stack. On guitar, chords shape.

What to Learn

Chords are built as hand shapes, not individual notes.

These shapes repeat up the neck (unlike the piano).

Patterns make everything easier — especially for someone who already knows harmony.

Starter Exercise

Pick a chord you know (G, C, D, Am). Slide it up the neck while keeping the shape. Hear how it becomes new chords instantly.

Key Resources

CAGED overview (Paul Davids): https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1p0yhiJdPRY

JustinGuitar “Beginner Mindset” lessons: https://www.justinguitar.com/categories/beginner-guitar-lessons-grade-1

Module 2

The CAGED System — Your New Map of the Guitar

Core Principle: Five shapes = every chord, everywhere.

If Luke learns these five shapes, he unlocks the entire fretboard:

C shape

A shape

G shape

E shape

D shape

Every major chord, every barre chord, every voicing… all come from these.

Why This Is Perfect for a Pianist

CAGED is the guitar’s “logic.” Once you see it, you can play the instrument instead of chasing it.

Key Resources

CAGED Deep Dive (Sean Daniel): https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9bqVdF4VQWc

JustinGuitar CAGED Essentials (free course): https://www.justinguitar.com/modules/caged-system

Module 3

Chord Families — The Guitarist’s Harmonic Shortcuts

Core Principle: Most songs use three chord families.

Luke already understands functional harmony. These three families will feel natural:

The Big Three:

G Family: G – C – D – Em

C Family: C – F – G – Am

D Family: D – G – A – Bm

Learn these, and he can play thousands of acoustic songs instantly.

Try This

Choose a song he knows on piano in G major. Play it on guitar using only G-family shapes. Instant fluency.

Key Resources

Andy Guitar Easy Chords & Songs: https://www.andyguitar.co.uk/online-lessons/categories/beginners

Module 4

Strumming — Turning Chords Into Music

Core Principle: Rhythm makes the song feel alive.

The trick to sounding “like a guitarist” is not the chords… It’s the right hand.

Learn These First:

Down, down-up, up-down-up (the universal pattern)

16th-note feel (for pop)

Shuffle feel (for folk & blues)

Exercise

Mute the strings with your left hand. Practice strumming patterns without chords to master the groove.

Key Resources

Marty Music Strumming Playlist: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL-RYb_OMw7GdHGwEJdBDEu79SUZlQO7Rh

JustinGuitar Strumming Techniques: https://www.justinguitar.com/modules/strumming-techniques

Module 5

Ear-Based Guitar — Translating Your Piano Intuition

Core Principle: Luke already knows how to do this — guitar just adds shapes.

What to Practice

Hear the interval → find it on one string

Turn those intervals into chords

Recognize common progressions (I–V–vi–IV, ii–V–I)

Why He’ll Learn Fast

Because the shapes repeat, he only needs to find something once for it to work everywhere.

Key Resources

Functional Ear Trainer App: https://www.matthewvancise.com/fet

JustinGuitar Ear Training: https://www.justinguitar.com/categories/ear-training

Module 6

Tabs — The Guitarist’s Sheet Music

Core Principle: Guitarists read grids, not staff notation.

TAB is easy:

6 lines = 6 strings

Numbers = fret numbers

Luke will pick this up instantly.

The Fretboard — Quick Navigation Tricks

Core Principle: The low E and A strings are your anchors.

Learn These First:

Notes on E string (open → 12th)

Notes on A string

Power chord shapes

Barre chord shapes (CAGED again)

Once Luke knows the E and A strings, the whole fretboard unlocks.

Module 10

Practice Design — How to Level Up Fast

Core Principle: 20 minutes of focused practice beats 2 hours of wandering.

Sample 20-Minute Routine

5 minutes – chord changes (G–C–D, C–F–G, etc.) 5 minutes – strumming pattern focus 5 minutes – melody or riff via tab 5 minutes – play a song you love

Bonus: Use Play-Along Tools

YouTube speed control

Songsterr slowdown function

Fender Play tempo tools (optional but good)

Key Takeaways

Guitar is shapes, patterns, and repetition — not note-reading.

Because Luke knows chords and harmony, he will learn much faster than beginners.

CAGED + chord families = instant fluency.

Strumming is 80% of sounding like a guitarist.

Play songs early and often — it accelerates everything.

Practice small, consistent sessions, not marathons.

The real goal: freedom — the ability to hear something and play it.
