Module 1

MODULE 1 — The Secret Power of the Dad Joke

Why Groan-Inducing Humor Actually Works

Core concepts

  • Disarms tension — a pun is a “soft landing” for conversation.
  • Signals approachability — you don’t take yourself too seriously.
  • Creates a shared moment — the eye-roll + smile combo is bonding.
  • Builds confidence — timing and voice get trained through repetition.
  • Teaches structure — setup → twist → reaction.

Mini Case Study — Coach K’s “Light Moment” Principle
Great leaders often open with a moment of levity to lower defenses and build trust. Dad jokes are the playful version of that same strategy.

Module 2

MODULE 2 — The Dad Joke Blueprint

The Anatomy of a Perfect (Terrible) Joke

A classic dad joke has three parts:

  • The Setup: serious face, normal question or statement.
  • The Misdirection: your audience predicts a sensible ending.
  • The Twist: the pun lands, logic collapses, joy begins.

Formula: Setup → Expectation → Twist that makes no sense but still makes sense.

Example:
“Why don’t eggs tell jokes?”
Because they’d crack each other up.

MODULE 3 — The Five Sacred Dad Joke Archetypes

Your complete taxonomy of groan

1. The Classic Pun
“I’m reading a book on anti-gravity. It’s impossible to put down.”

2. The Question/Answer Groaner
“Why did the scarecrow win an award?”
He was outstanding in his field.

3. The Literal Interpretation Joke
“My boss told me to have a good day… so I went home.”

4. The Overly-Confident Misunderstanding
“I used to play piano by ear… now I use my hands.”

5. The Unexpected Wisdom Joke
“Never trust atoms. They make up everything.”

Module 4

MODULE 4 — How to Deliver Dad Jokes Like a Pro

Timing, Expression, and Maximum Groan Factor

Delivery rules

  • Deliver deadpan (as if it’s objectively brilliant).
  • Hold eye contact for half a beat after the punchline.
  • Groans count as success — they’re applause with personality.
  • Use the environment: objects around you = instant material.

Example:
Waiter: “How’s the food?”
You: “It’s pasta-tively amazing.”

Module 5

MODULE 5 — Write Your Own Dad Jokes

A Practical Framework

4-step recipe

  • Step 1: Pick a normal object (stairs, pencils, clouds, shoes).
  • Step 2: List verbs/ideas associated with it.
  • Step 3: Find double meanings / sound-alikes.
  • Step 4: Combine into a twist.

Example creation process:
Object: stairs → idea: “up to something”
Joke: “Why don’t you trust stairs? They’re always up to something.”

Module 7

MODULE 7 — Advanced Techniques: Situational Dad Jokes

Humor as a Leadership Skill (but silly)

Ready-to-deploy lines

  • Meeting running long: “I’d like to wrap up… but I forgot the wrapping paper.”
  • Compliment: “Aw shucks… and I didn’t even wear my shucking gloves.”
  • Track teammates: “I tried a marathon once… but it was too fast paced.”
  • Guitar practice: “This chord progression is major news.”
  • Cold day: “I’m not built for this weather… I’m two-ply at best.”

The key is context. The best dad jokes feel like they were “found” in the moment, not pulled from a script.
