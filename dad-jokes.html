Why groan-inducing humor actually works

Core concepts

  • Disarms tension — A pun is a “soft landing” for any conversation.
  • Signals approachability — It shows you don’t take yourself too seriously.
  • Creates a shared moment — People bond through the little eye-roll + smile combo.

MODULE 2 — The Dad Joke Blueprint

The anatomy of a perfect (terrible) joke

A classic dad joke has three parts:

  • The Setup (Serious Face Engaged)
    A normal-sounding question or statement.
  • The Misdirection (The Audience Leans In)
    They think you’re going one direction.
  • The Twist (Certified Groan Activation)
    A pun, unexpected meaning, or absurd logic.

Formula

  • Setup → Expectation → Twist That Makes No Sense but Still Makes Sense

Example:
“Why don’t eggs tell jokes?”
Because they’d crack each other up.

Practice

  • Write 3 setups for the same object (e.g., “stairs,” “pizza,” “cloud”).
  • Pick the best setup and try 2 different twists.
Module 3

MODULE 3 — The Five Sacred Dad Joke Archetypes

Your taxonomy of groan (and how to use it)

1) The Classic Pun

  • Wordplay that gently hurts the brain.
  • Example: “I’m reading a book on anti-gravity. It’s impossible to put down.”

2) The Question/Answer Groaner

  • Example: “Why did the scarecrow win an award?”
    He was outstanding in his field.

3) The Literal Interpretation Joke

  • Take a phrase literally and let logic collapse.
  • Example: “My boss told me to have a good day… so I went home.”

4) The Overly-Confident Misunderstanding

  • Say something wrong with absolute confidence.
  • Example: “I used to play piano by ear… now I use my hands.”

5) The Unexpected Wisdom Joke

  • Surprisingly profound… but still dumb.
  • Example: “Never trust atoms. They make up everything.”

Mini-challenge

  • Create one joke in each archetype using the same theme (food, sports, school, music).
  • Deliver them in different moods: deadpan, excited, whisper, “serious lecture.”
Module 4

MODULE 4 — How to Deliver Dad Jokes Like a Pro

Timing, expression, and maximum groan factor

Delivery rules

  • 1) Say it completely deadpan
    Like you genuinely think this joke is brilliant.
  • 2) Hold eye contact for 0.5 seconds
    Allows the joke to land (or crash).
  • 3) Accept laughter and groans as victory
    The groan is the applause.
  • 4) Use the environment
    See objects and create on-the-fly puns.

Example:
At dinner, waiter asks, “How’s the food?”
You answer: “It’s pasta-tively amazing.”

Performance drill

  • Tell the same joke 3 ways: deadpan, overly dramatic, calm professor.
  • Notice: delivery changes how people respond more than the joke itself.
Module 5

MODULE 5 — Write Your Own Dad Jokes

A practical framework (repeatable forever)

Steps

  • Step 1 — Pick a normal object
    (e.g., stairs, pencils, clouds, shoes)
  • Step 2 — List verbs / ideas associated with it
    Stairs: climb, fall, step, elevate, up/down.
  • Step 3 — Find double meanings
    “Step up,” “falling down,” “high vs. low.”
  • Step 4 — Combine into a twist

Example creation process

  • Object: stairs
  • Associated idea: they’re always up to something
  • Joke: “Why don’t you trust stairs? They’re always up to something.”

Boom — instant dad joke.

Weekly challenge

  • Write 10 original dad jokes.
  • Test them on family members.
  • Keep the ones that earn the strongest groan.

Module 7

MODULE 7 — Advanced Techniques: Situational Dad Jokes

Humor as a leadership skill (but silly)

Situational deployments

  • Meeting Running Long:
    “I’d like to wrap up… but I forgot the wrapping paper.”
  • Someone Compliments Your Skills:
    “Aw shucks… and I didn’t even wear my shucking gloves.”
  • Around track teammates:
    “I tried running a marathon once… but it was too fast paced.”
  • At guitar practice:
    “This chord progression is major news.”
  • On a cold day:
    “I’m not built for this weather… I’m two-ply at best.”

Improv rule

  • Notice something in the room.
  • Say the first clean double-meaning that appears.
  • Deliver it confidently, like it’s a public service.
